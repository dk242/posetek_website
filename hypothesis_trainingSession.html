How It Works

Four Steps to Better Performance

The same biomechanical pipeline as our performance diagnostic — jump and kick analysis, weak-link identification, and a periodized plan — delivered after a quick capture at the end of practice.

Step 01

Practice Like Normal

Train with your team like you always do. After practice wraps up, your coach runs a quick 5-minute data capture using our cameras and performance tags — covering your vertical jump and ball-striking mechanics. No disruption to your session.

Step 02

We Analyze Everything

Our system processes your data using the same biomechanical analysis as our in-person performance testing. For your jump, we break down loading mechanics, takeoff angles, and explosive power. For your kick, we quantify plant-foot positioning, hip rotation, backswing, and follow-through — pinpointing exactly where technique is costing you.

Step 03

Results on Your Dashboard

Within 2 days, your full results are live on your personal dashboard — efficiency scores, weak-link identification, and a personalized improvement plan with specific coaching cues and corrective drills. Built by a Stanford-trained mechanical engineer, former D1 soccer player, and our proprietary AI system.

Step 04

Come Back & See the Difference

Three weeks later, your coach runs the same quick capture at practice. We quantify exactly how your jump and kick mechanics have improved — side-by-side with your first test — and fine-tune your program for the next phase. Bundle pricing saves you money vs. booking follow-up separately (same structure as our in-facility follow-up).

What We Analyze

Athleticism Distilled for Soccer

Every metric is tied to on-field performance — how you move, how efficiently you generate power, and where technique is costing you.

Jump

Explosive Power, Optimized

Not just "jump higher." We analyze loading mechanics, takeoff angles, and landing patterns to build power that translates to headers, duels, and changing direction — same methodology as our performance diagnostic.

Sprint

Speed That Translates

Stride angle, ground contact time, hip extension, and acceleration posture — the biomechanical numbers behind your speed. See exactly what your sprint mechanics look like and where to find more yards.

Kick

Strike with Precision & Power

We quantify plant-foot positioning, hip rotation, and follow-through. Harder, more accurate shots and passes — with less injury risk from poor form.
