Kicking power

Ball speed, hip rotation, follow-through. Know your strike.

› Learn more

Every elite striker shares a common thread: they move their body as one connected kinetic chain. Energy starts at the plant knee, travels up through the hips, transfers into the striking knee, and finally releases through the striking hip into the ball.

Hitting with real power isn't about swinging harder — it's about the small details. How you leverage your upper body, the timing between each joint firing, and how well you stabilize through contact all decide how cleanly that energy reaches the ball.

PoseTek quantifies those details and tracks how they evolve over time, so you can see exactly how your strike is getting more efficient session by session.

⚡

Sprint speed & acceleration

Top speed, 0–10m burst, stride mechanics — all from video.

› Learn more

Jumping on a counter, closing down space as a defender, recovering into your shape, or blowing past your mark — every one of those moments lives or dies on your first step and how quickly you reach top speed.

PoseTek quantifies exactly where you stand on acceleration and max velocity, then tracks how those numbers grow over time so you know the work on the pitch and in the gym is actually translating to faster soccer.

📈

Jump mechanics & height

Quantify vertical and explosive power. Track every session.

› Learn more

A jump is one of the cleanest ways to measure how much power you're producing and how well your joints work together to produce it. The way your hips, knees, ankles, and upper body coordinate on takeoff and landing tells a deeper story than a number on a leaderboard.

Those same mechanics show up everywhere else in your game — decelerating into a tackle, accelerating out of a turn, holding posture while you pass or defend. Cleaner jump mechanics translate directly to more efficient, safer movement on the pitch, and PoseTek shows you where to sharpen them.

Who's behind it

Former D1 soccer player. Stanford-trained engineer. Biomechanics nerd.

After tearing my ACL as captain of my division 1 college team (Lehigh University) — and then the same knee's meniscus a year later — I leaned on my mechanical engineering background to understand exactly how my body moved. PoseTek grew out of that obsession: a tool to train safer, move better, and optimize the mechanics that actually matter on the pitch.

› Read the full story

I was captain of my Division I team at Lehigh University when I tore my ACL in the first game of the soccer season. The following summer — right before heading to Stanford for grad school — I tore the meniscus in that same knee.

Those injuries forced a new perspective. With a background in mechanical engineering, I became fascinated with how my body actually moved: how to generate force, rotate, land, and strike in ways that kept me safe and still performed at the level the game demanded.

As I rehabbed and worked my way back, I started tracking my performance and obsessively refining the movements specific to soccer — plant foot, hip rotation, contact point, deceleration. PoseTek is the product of that journey: the tool I wish I'd had when I first stepped back onto the field, now built for every player who wants to understand their body the same way.
